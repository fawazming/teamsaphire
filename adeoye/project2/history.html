History of LLMs

The history of Large Language Models (LLMs) is a story of rapid progress and innovation in the field of natural language processing (NLP) . The development of LLMs has been a long journey, spanning several decades. In this overview, we will explore the key milestones and advancements that have led to the creation of modern LLMs.

Early Beginnings (1960s-1980s)

The first NLP systems were rule-based relying on hand-crafted rules to process language. These systems were limited in their capabilities and were often brittle, meaning they could only handle a narrow range of inputs.

Rise of Machine Learning (1990s-2000s)

The 1990s and 2000s saw the rise of machine learning in NLP. Machine learning techniques like decision trees,Support Vector Machines (SVMs), and neural networks started to gain traction in NLP.

Deep Learning Revolution (2010s)

The 2010s saw a deep learning revolution in NLP. Deep learning techniques like Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Transformers revolutionized NLP

Large Language Models (2020s)

In the 2020s, LLMs have become a major area of research in NLP. LLMs are transformer-based models that have achieved state-of-the-art results in many NLP tasks.

The development of large language models (LLMs) has been a long journey. Key milestones include:

Early Beginnings

  1. IBM Alignment Models: Pioneered statistical language modeling.
  2. Smoothed N-Gram Model: Achieved state-of-the-art perplexity

Rise of Neural Networks

  1. Neural Networks in Image Processing: Became dominant
  2. Google's Neural Machine Translation : Converted to neural machine translation.

Recent Developments

  1. GPT-3 and Its Successors: Demonstrated improved accuracy and multimodal capabilities
  2. ChatGPT and Its Impact: Captured the imagination of the general population.
  3. Open-Source Models: Source-available models gained popularity.
